Climate and Energy Policy Research Lab (CEPRL)

Overview

Incubated at the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur, the Climate and Energy Policy Research (CEPRL) Lab is an interdisciplinary research-based initiative to address the global climate crisis. It aims to bring together international research collaborators from diverse fields- academia, policy-making, research organizations, think-tanks, NGOs, and civil societies- to engage in fruitful network-building and knowledge exchange processes. The lab's ongoing projects involve air pollution, climate change policy networks, just energy transitions, and grassroots innovations. Our research uses data collected through surveys, ethnographic research, policy reports, and online media repositories and combines classical and computational sociological methods for data refinement and analysis.

CEPRL is currently working with the research teams from Australia, Brazil, Japan, and the United States. CEPRL's team has published in peer-reviewed journals, including the British Journal of Sociology and Current Sociology.

POST-DOCTORAL STUDENT

Avinash Kumar

Avinash Kumar is an ICSSR Post-Doctoral Fellow at the Climate and Energy Policy Research Lab (CEPRL) at IIT Kanpur. He completed his Masters in Development Studies and PhD in Sociology of Science and Technology from the Department of Humanities and Social Sciences, Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati. He was appointed as a research fellow at the Institute for Advanced Studies on Science, Technology and Society (IAS-STS), Graz – Austria, from October 1, 2016, to June 30, 2017.

With research interests in Science, Technology and Society, Information and Communication Technologies, Scientific Collaborations and Social Network Analysis, Dr. Kumar has been extensively engaged in exploring the structure, pattern, and function of networks, particularly scientific, social and policy networks, using a combination of theoretical and empirical methods situated within the Sociology of Science and Technology. His ideas have found place in journals such as Perspectives on Global Development and Technology, Asian Journal of Social Science, and Journal of Scientometrics Research.

Currently Dr Kumar is working on COMPON. a transnational project that studies climate change policy networks and media discourse networks in 20 countries. He is critically involved in analysing the empirical data for India and comparing them with the cases of Australia, Japan and Brazil.

PhD STUDENTS

Bhavna Joshi

Tracing her academic journey through multiple fields including life sciences, biotechnology, forensic science, and anthropology, Bhavna Joshi is currently working on the problem of air pollution in Delhi using a sociological perspective. Her work engages with three broad themes- knowledge production, risk perception, and energy justice, and delves to decipher the instruments of construction and assertion of knowledge through discourses and myriad risk perceiving positions of various actors within the justice framework of the problem.

Bhavna qualified the National Eligibility Test for Junior Research Fellowship offered by the University Grants Commission of India in 2015, and is currently receiving institute fellowship for her doctoral work. She completed her MSc. and M.Phil. degree in Anthropology from University of Delhi and explored the socio-cultural connotations of ‘home’ and ‘homelessness’ through her ethnographic work in the urban and rural India. Her paper, co-authored with Prof. Swarnakar, titled, ‘Staying away, staying alive: Exploring risk and stigma of COVID-19 in the context of beliefs, actors and hierarchies in India’ got accepted for publication by Current Sociology in December 2020.

Rajshri Shukla

Rajshri Shukla is a second-year doctoral candidate with her research interests spanning Environment Sociology, Climate Change, Energy Transition, Energy Justice, and Social Stratification. In her thesis, she is particularly trying to explore the treatment of the structural inequalities and injustices in the process of energy transition towards renewable energy by conducting a comparative study between India and Australia. She holds a national-level Junior Research Fellowship funded by the University Grants Commission.

She completed her B.A. (Hons.) in Sociology from Hindu College, Delhi University, and M.A. (Sociology) from Kanpur University. In 2018, she worked as a lecturer at College of Law, Pranveer Singh Institute of Technology, Kanpur for five months. She presented her work at the International Workshop on Socio-ecological relations of the energy transition in Australia, Germany, and India and European Consortium for Political Research (ECPR) General Conference 2020.

Peter Dichun Rwangmei

Peter’s doctoral work explores the intersection of environmental sociology and indigenous religion to combat environmental crisis in a sustainable way. He is currently conducting his fieldwork among the Rongmei tribe of Manipur covering both the urban and rural regions.

Peter completed his Masters in Sociology from Panjab University in 2013.

Pritha Sarkar

Pritha’s doctoral work focuses on the Grassroots Innovations (GIs) related to Local Low Carbon Emission Initiatives (LLCEIs) in India, where she seeks to explore the role of LLCEI in energy transition and the factors affecting the growth, development or impediments of LLCEIs, along with the contribution of Indian statutes for the same.

She holds a Master of Arts (M.A) in Sociology from University of Kalyani, where she conducted a sociological study on the level of awareness about gender role stereotype among married women in Barrackpore. Prior to joining IIT Kanpur, Pritha’s work experience spanned diverse areas including banking sector, welfare organisations, and teaching positions in schools and colleges.

Swagata Das

Swagata’s interest in environmental sociology drove her to explore the Forest Policy Network in India in her doctoral work. This study is important to understand the actor negotiation and power governance within the broad contour of the forest policy network at the domestic level.

She holds a Masters in Sociology from West Bengal State University and Bachelors of Arts in Sociology from Kalyani University.

Naorem Nishikanta Mangang

The growing concerns of climate change in the world motivated Nishi to work on the policy framework of energy transition in India. He is currently getting a Junior Research Fellowship by University Grants Commission for his doctoral research.

Prior to joining IIT Kanpur, he completed M.Phil in Sociology from Jadavpur University and obtained M.A. Sociology degree from Pondicherry University. In 2019, he presented a paper titled “Market-a space for social mobility and identity assertion for the women in Manipur” at National seminar on “Regimes of Gender in Contemporary Globalization” organised by Department of Political science, Presidency University Kolkata.

Haritha S Narayanan

Haritha is a post graduate in Politics and International Relations from Pondicherry University. She is currently interning with Prof. Swarnakar at IIT Kanpur to explore the role of NGOs in the International and National Climate Movement and Advocacy. She has been reviewing literature, collecting data and writing summaries on the climatic issues happening around the globe. As an aspiring student of politics and international relations, her research interest further includes the understanding of the fields of human development with a focus on social policy and planning, South Asia, Maritime studies and Human rights. She holds a B.A (Honours) degree in Political Science from Gargi College, University of Delhi. She was also associated with the Council for Social Development (CSD), New Delhi and Manohar Parrikar Institute for Defence Studies and Analyses (MP- IDSA), New Delhi as a research intern.
